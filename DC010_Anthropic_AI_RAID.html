Investment Thesis

Anthropic's $50B infrastructure commitment represents its first major independent data center initiative beyond cloud provider relationships. The partnership with Fluidstack (UK-based neocloud) deploys capacity through third-party infrastructure owners rather than direct ownership. Four confirmed sites exceed 1 GW total capacity with multi-gigawatt expansion potential. Lake Mariner (NY, 360 MW, TeraWulf) comes online H1 2026 at a former Somerset coal plant drawing 89-90% zero-carbon grid power. Texas sites at Abernathy (168 MW, TeraWulf JV) and Barber Lake (300 MW, Cipher Mining) follow in H2 2026. River Bend (LA, 245 MW expandable to 2.3 GW, Hut 8) activates Q2 2027. Anthropic raised $33.7B since 2021 and reached $7B annualized revenue by October 2025, targeting break-even by 2028. Google provides multi-billion dollar backstop guarantees for Fluidstack lease obligations. The structure gives Anthropic compute access without capital burden of direct facility ownership, but elevates partner execution risk. Fluidstack has never executed at this scale. Scale gap with competitors remains substantial as Anthropic's $50B is 10x smaller than OpenAI Stargate ($500B). (Sources: Anthropic Nov 2025 announcement; TeraWulf investor relations; Hut 8 Dec 2025 release; Crunchbase)
