Key Risks

Investment Thesis

Intel Ohio was announced as a transformational $20B investment in 2022, later expanded to $28B to establish a leading-edge semiconductor manufacturing hub. However, the project faces severe challenges. The original 2025 production timeline has slipped to 2030-31, a 5-6 year delay. Intel has failed to secure any external foundry customers despite years of effort. The company's financial distress, with significant losses and workforce reductions, raises questions about project viability. The $7.86B CHIPS Act award includes milestone requirements that may not be met, risking clawback. Ohio's lack of semiconductor workforce infrastructure compounds execution challenges. While the strategic importance remains high, Intel's ability to execute is severely compromised.
