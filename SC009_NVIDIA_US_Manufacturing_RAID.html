Investment Thesis

NVIDIA's US manufacturing initiative represents the first domestic assembly of AI supercomputers. Announced April 14, 2025, the project positions NVIDIA products for "Made in USA" designation without NVIDIA directly owning manufacturing facilities. The $500B figure widely reported represents projected AI infrastructure sales value over four years, not NVIDIA capital expenditure. NVIDIA remains "fabless" by design, relying on contract manufacturing partners. Foxconn acquired 1.03M sq ft in Houston for $142M plus a secondary 200K sq ft facility, committing $450M total with 600 direct jobs. Wistron announced $761M for two Fort Worth facilities (800+ jobs, H1 2026 operational). Both facilities assemble GB300 NVL72 systems containing 72 Blackwell Ultra GPUs, 36 Grace CPUs, and approximately 5,000 cables spanning two miles per rack. Critical chip fabrication remains at TSMC (Taiwan and Arizona), with advanced packaging (CoWoS) still largely Taiwan-based. The move is driven by supply chain diversification and export control compliance rather than tariff avoidance (semiconductors currently exempt). Jensen Huang called it a milestone for AI infrastructure production in the United States. (Sources: NVIDIA April 2025 announcement; Foxconn/Wistron investor materials; Fort Worth Report; Houston Chronicle)
